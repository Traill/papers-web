About TrailHead

Trailhead is your own personal conference scheduler. To make it easier for you to find presentations that you care about you can search for people or topics and browse connections between articles. Whatever you find interesting you can add to your own schedule and download it as a pdf or add it to your calendar.

So dive in and click away! Search for a topic you like and use the graph to browse between related talks. Any time you find something that interest you, go ahead and add it to your schedule to get your own personal exquisite conference program.

Should you later decide to add or drop a few talks, despair not. Trailhead lets you save your schedule to come back later. If you are using the same computer it's as simple as reopening the page later, and the schedule will be restored. In case you want to transfer the information to another computer, click on 'Save Graph' in the top right corner to create your own personal graph.

Get Started

Graph Legend

  • An article
  • An article you have already added to your schedule
  • The last article you selected
  • An article matching your search term

Navigation

  • Move by dragging the graph with your mouse
  • Zoom by scrolling in or out
  • Add an article to your schedule
  • Remove an article from your schedule

Schedule

  • Begin by clicking on an article in the graph and add it to your schedule
  • Download your schedule as a PDF ready to print
  • Remove all articles from your schedule

Other use

Search: Trailhead lets you work with advanced search features. You can select different criterions to search nodes in the graph like time or location of presentation. The filters are saved in the panel and you can toggle and untoggle them.

Schedule: When you're happy with the schedule you have built in the panel, you can either download a pdf with all the information about the talks you selected or an ical file. An ical file lets you add events in most calendar applications. If you want to sync it with your smartphone you should follow these steps:

  1. Create a new calendar on your computer that syncs with your smartphone.
  2. Add the ical events on this calendar.
  3. Connect your smartphone and sync.

      4. Alternatively, you can send the ical file by mail and open the file on your iphone mail app. If you make some changes later, you can easily remove the old schedule in your calendar and add the new dates and times to your calendar

      How does it work?

      The graph you are looking at is generated by comparing the closeness of a bit more than 400 papers submitted to the 2013 ITA. They are currently rated based on a naive bayesian textual comparison of the similarity of the word frequencies of the submittet research.

      This project is developed with the help of a series of great tools. The graph is based on D3, a javascript library for creating SVG graphics. The javascript interface is based on Curl, Radio and many more. Check the code out on Github for a full overview.
